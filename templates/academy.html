Module I

The Macroeconomics of Inflation

Holding fiat currency (rupees, dollars) is a mathematically guaranteed loss of purchasing power over time. Central banks systematically inject liquidity into the economy, decreasing the discrete value of every circulating unit.

The Imperative: Investing is not an optional "risk" you take to get rich; it is a mandatory defensive structure required to maintain your current standard of living. If the true inflation rate is 6%, any capital sitting in a savings account yielding 3% is eroding in real purchasing power.

Equation: Real Return = Nominal Return - Inflation Rate.
# A 10% stock market return during 6% inflation yields a marginal 4% true wealth increase.
Module II

Market Mechanics & Valuations

Equity fundamentally represents fractional ownership of a corporation's future Free Cash Flows (FCF). It is not a magical chart.

  • Order Books & Liquidity Prices change via the Bid-Ask spread. There is no central price algorithm; prices only move because a buyer physically executes an order against an opposing seller's limit order, consuming "liquidity" on the book.
  • Market Capitalization Price × Outstanding Shares. This dictates the actual size of the company. A ₹10 stock with 1 billion shares is inherently a larger company than a ₹5,000 stock with 1 million shares.

Module III

Behavioral Finance

The math is easy. The psychology is what destroys retail traders. Cognitive biases force humans to buy exactly at the top of bubbles (greed) and capitulate at the exact market bottoms (fear).

Loss Aversion

Psychologically, the pain of losing ₹100,000 feels twice as intense as the joy of gaining ₹100,000. This causes investors to panic sell structurally sound assets during temporary market corrections, crystallizing what was only a paper loss into permanent damage.

Confirmation Bias

Investors seek out information (news, forums) that confirms what they already believe about a stock, actively ignoring massive red flags in financial statements. The market doesn't care about your opinion; respect the data.
